## Draft — email to Alex Towell

Subject: Oncofiles ← Chartfold architecture borrow — hello from the EU side

Hi Alex,

Peter Fusek from Bratislava, Slovakia. I maintain oncofiles.com — an
open-source (MIT, Python, stdio/HTTP) MCP server that lets cancer patients and
caregivers connect their Google Drive to Claude.ai / ChatGPT and query their
own medical documents. Five live patients today, mostly SK + CZ.

Reason for the email: I shipped a canonical clinical-record data model this
week (table `clinical_records` with a `record_type` discriminator, full audit
trail, notes and analyses as separate peer tables, every row carries `source`
/ `session_id` / `caller_identity`). The design is a direct distillation of
Chartfold's 17-table schema into a single-table-plus-discriminator form — keeps
the expressiveness without the join count. Your separation of `notes` (human)
and `analyses` (AI) in particular shaped the whole shape of it.

Three reasons I wanted to say hi directly:

1. **Arkiv export compatibility.** If Oncofiles can export to Arkiv's format
   (or a superset thereof), patients get zero-friction portability between our
   two projects. That's a real trust-building feature, not a marketing line.

2. **FHIR ingestion.** Chartfold already parses athenahealth/Epic exports.
   Oncofiles parses SK hospital PDFs with AI-OCR. Different inbound realities,
   but a shared output shape (clinical_records) could let either project serve
   as the other's parser if the use case calls for it.

3. **Cross-positioning.** ChatGPT Health launched excluding EEA/CH/UK;
   HealthEx is US-only via Anthropic. There's a genuine geographic partition
   in patient-side oncology AI that we're both well-placed for (you US, me
   EU). Cross-linking our READMEs + landing pages would help both projects
   surface to the other continent's users.

Would you be open to a 30-min call in the next two weeks? I'd love to hear
where Chartfold is heading, share what I ran into building the SK oncology
integration, and see if the clinical_records export shape is close enough to
Arkiv to be worth formalizing.

Happy to work around your timezone (I'm UTC+1/+2).

Best,
Peter Fusek
peter.fusek@instarea.sk
