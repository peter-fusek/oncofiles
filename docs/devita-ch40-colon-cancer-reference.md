## 1. Adjuvant Treatment (Stage II/III) — pp. 642-647

### FOLFOX Regimen
- **FOLFOX-4**: 5-yr DFS 73.3% vs 67.4% (LV5FU2 alone), HR=0.80, 95% CI
- **mFOLFOX-6**: Standard of care (not FOLFOX-4) — routinely used due to greater convenience
- Modified FOLFOX-6 regimen: basis for all FOLFOX-based NCI Intergroup adjuvant and metastatic trials
- **IDEA trial**: 3 months of total treatment appropriate for most stage III patients
- FOLFOX or Cape/Ox: regimens of choice for stage III colon cancer (absent contraindications)
- Irinotecan-based regimens: NOT for adjuvant (increased toxicity, no long-term benefit)

### Toxicity Profile
- Grade 3/4 neutropenia: 41% (FOLFOX-4 arm)
- Peripheral neuropathy: grade 2 in ~31% of FOLFOX patients
- **w-3 PUFAs**: 600 mg 3x/day during chemotherapy — significant reduction in PN incidence/severity, improved motor/nerve conduction (Zhang et al, double-blind RCT, 179 pts on Cap Ox x6 cycles)
- Long-term neuropathy morbidity increasingly appreciated; risk stratification strategies emerging
- 3 vs 6 months oxaliplatin: data have failed to show definitive noninferiority of 3 months vs 6 months

### Biologic Agents in Adjuvant Setting — ALL NEGATIVE
- **Bevacizumab + mFOLFOX-6**: Fully negative trial — no DFS or OS improvement at 5-yr follow-up. Bev delayed micrometastasis progression but did NOT eradicate them. AVANT trial: bev-containing arms trended INFERIOR to FOLFOX-alone.
- **Bevacizumab + capecitabine (QUASAR 2)**: Negative outcome direction
- **Cetuximab + mFOLFOX-6**: No benefit even in KRAS wild-type. FOLFOX-alone trended better.
- **Conclusion**: Bevacizumab and anti-EGFR agents should NOT be used in adjuvant setting

## 2. MSI/MSS Status and Immunotherapy — p. 646

### MSI Prevalence by Stage
- Stage II: 22% MSI-H
- Stage III: 12% MSI-H
- Stage IV: only 3.5% MSI-H
- MSI-H is a strong favorable prognostic factor in stage II

### Treatment Implications
- **MSI-H + Stage II**: Should NOT be treated with fluoropyrimidines alone
- **MSI-H + metastatic**: Immune checkpoint inhibitors show meaningful activity
- **MSS (pMMR) + metastatic**: ICB has NOT shown activity. Several small studies suggested activity but larger confirmatory trials FAILED. "Further investigation needed to identify subgroups, if any, of MSS that will benefit from immunotherapy."
- **MSI-H + Stage III**: Checkpoint inhibitors in adjuvant setting NOT established, should be investigational only

### Patient Applicability
- Patient is pMMR/MSS → checkpoint monotherapy NOT indicated (confirmed by DeVita 12e)
- MSS immunotherapy combinations (e.g., botensilimab+balstilimab) remain investigational

## 3. Circulating Tumor DNA (ctDNA) — p. 646

- Detectable in bloodstream of almost all patients with advanced CRC
- Important prognostic tool; may enable more effective decision making
- Has outperformed many existing clinicopathologic risk factors as surrogate biomarker for minimal residual disease
- Potential role in tailoring treatment strategies where established protocols fall short

## 4. First-line Metastatic CRC Treatment — pp. 654-660

### mFOLFOX-6 Regimen (Standard)
- Oxaliplatin 85 mg/m²
- Simplified LV5FU2 at 2,400 mg/m² over 46-48 hours (1,200 mg/m²/day x 2 consecutive days)
- Bolus 5-FU deleted
- **OPTIMOX strategy**: Discontinue oxaliplatin after 3 months (12 weeks), planned reintroduction after 12 weeks or sooner if progression

### FOLFOX vs FOLFIRI (First-line equivalence)
- Tournigand et al: Phase 3, FOLFOX-6 vs FOLFIRI — striking consistency between regimens
- Either FOLFOX-6 or FOLFIRI acceptable as first-line treatment
- Larger trial (360 pts): FOLFOX-4 vs FOLFIRI — comparable efficacy, differing toxicity profiles

### Sequential vs Combination Chemotherapy (CAIRO trial)
- Sequential (Cape → Iri → Cape/Ox): OS 16.3 mo, 1-yr 64%, PFS 5.8m, RR 20%
- Combination (Cape/Iri → Cape/Ox): OS 17.4 mo, 1-yr 67%, PFS 7.8m, RR 41%
- OS difference not significant (P=0.33); PFS and RR significantly better with combination
- Conclusion: No compelling evidence that indefinite chemotherapy is necessary; discontinuation after reasonable period is appropriate

## 5. Bevacizumab in Metastatic CRC — pp. 659-660

### Pivotal Trial: IFL + Bevacizumab (Table 40.17A)
| Regimen | N | Response Rate | PFS | OS |
|---------|---|---------------|-----|-----|
| IFL + placebo | 411 | 34.8% | 6.2 mo | 15.6 mo |
| IFL + bevacizumab | 402 | 44.8% (p=0.004) | 10.6 mo (p<0.001) | 20.3 mo (p<0.001) |

### FOLFOX-4 + Bevacizumab
- Modest but statistically significant OS improvement: 12.5 vs 10.7 months (P=0.0024)
- Grade 3/4 toxicities not increased
- Bevacizumab dose: 10 mg/kg
- Bevacizumab monotherapy: substantially inferior PFS/RR — should NOT be used alone

### NO16966 Trial (FOLFOX/CapeOx + Bevacizumab)
- 1,400 patients, previously untreated mCRC
- Two-by-two randomization: FOLFOX-4 or Cape/Ox + placebo or bevacizumab
- PFS: 9.4 vs 8.0 months (HR=0.83, P=0.003) — significant but more modest than IFL trial
- OS: 21.3 vs 19.9 months (HR=0.89, P=0.077) — approached but did NOT reach statistical significance
- No response rate benefit
- Many patients discontinued before progression (nonbevacizumab-related toxicity) — may have diminished impact

### Key Points for Patient
- Bevacizumab + FOLFOX: Established first-line metastatic CRC treatment
- NOT for adjuvant (stage II/III) — negative in all trials
- Bevacizumab continues to be used as first-line with FOLFOX despite modest OS benefit in NO16966

## 7. Liver Metastases (Ch 85 search snippets) — pp. 1662-1670

### First-line Treatment
- FOLFOX or FOLFIRI: standard combinatorial regimens
- FOLFOXIRI (FOLFOX + irinotecan): PFS 9.8 vs 6.8 months (P=0.026) — increased PFS
- Neoadjuvant FOLFOX: demonstrated marked regression in initially unresectable liver mets

### SIRT (Y90 Microspheres)
- SIRT + FOLFOX vs FOLFOX alone: ~549 vs 554 patients
- Median OS NOT improved by addition of SIRT
- Conclusion: SIRT + FOLFOX not recommended in unselected liver-dominant mCRC

### Surgical Margins
- Significance of margin size after neoadjuvant FOLFOX/FOLFIRI for colorectal liver metastases is actively studied

## 8. Surveillance After Definitive Treatment — p. 650

- CEA monitoring: every 3 months for first 3 years, then every 6 months years 4-5
- CT chest/abdomen: annually for first 3 years (some guidelines: every 6 months)
- Colonoscopy: 1 year post-surgery, then every 3-5 years
- Physical exam: every 3-6 months first 3 years, every 6 months years 4-5
- Liver function tests: NOT recommended for routine surveillance (CEA superior)
- CT scans + CEA should not be continued beyond 5 years
